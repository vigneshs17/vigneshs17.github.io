About me

Although I haven't figured that out yet, I will talk a little bit about myself. My name is Vignesh Srinivasan. I am perpetually curious; I like to know my things work and I love to solve problems.

As I mentioned above that I love solving problems, I decided to study something along thatline. I study computer science and engineering at National Institute of Technology, Karnataka, Surathkal. Prior to that, I completed my high school graduation at Al Khor International School, Al Khor, Qatar. When I don't have work and I am free, I swim or watch videos on YouTube. The Patriot Act is a really cool show. Do check it out if you're into comedy or a fan of Hasan Minhaj. I think Hasan Minjar is one of the best storytellers out there.

There are a lot of problems around us. But, we, more often that not fail to see them. Not because we do not know how to solve them, but because we aren't directly affected by it. For example, in college, we are often assigned programming tasks, that we should code and run it in front of the instructors. In a class of 60, this becomes a cumbersome task for as they can perhaps spend their time on something for fruitful. This is pretty common, but not everyone notices it because it isn't a burden for them. I wanted to change that. So, I soughted to solve that issue, but implementing an assignment grader in my college. All the students have to do is code it out and submit it. Boom! That's it! The grader takes of everything based on specifications mentioned by the instructor. Another small example. Every night, before I go to bed, I turn off Wi-Fi, BlueTooth and sometimes Mobile Data. I noticed that this took quite a few seconds and a few taps. So, I designmed a script that does everything that I want with literally a touch of a button. My point is, before you start out solving the big problems, it is good to solve the smaller ones as it gives you quicker results and a sense of accomplishment subsequently motivating you to do better.

Optical Image Recognition

This project aims at an implementation of a system that will take a input image with hand written number on a fragment of paper. The system will be designed to recognize these numbers and store them. The input would be from a handheld device like a mobile camera. The project deals with taking the input , normalizing aspect ratio and scaling, feature extraction and recognizing the number. We would be using kNN to implement it and the MNIST database. We achieved an accuracy of 80%.
